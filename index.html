facilities



school library

"When in doubt go to the library." -J.K. Rowling

Campion School has a spacious, well stocked library that caters to the children's need for reading and references and it is here that the children come in contact with the different authors and updated sources of information. Students of classes III to XII enjoy reading varieties of books and do reference work during their library periods and other times. A vast resourceful collection of books in different categories - Computer Science, Applied Science, Pure Science, Hindi, Malayalam, History, Geography, Religion and Philosophy are available. A section purely for references containing Encyclopedia, Gandhian books and dictionaries of many languages such as German, French, Sanskrit etc. are also available. Both students and faculty members copiously use the library convenience .



labs

"Innovation and renewal are required to keep a laboratory on the frontiers of science." -Burton Richter

All the labs are well furnished and equipped to the international specification and standards. Computer studies are an integral part of the school curriculum from class IV. Computer classes are organized to equip students with computer language and technology for advanced computer learning later. Computer Laboratory, Science laboratories for Physics, Chemistry, Biology from the necessary infrastructure for the acquisition and experimentation of scientific information and knowledge.



Auditorium

"There is that smaller world which is the stage, and that larger stage which is the world." -Isaac Goldberg

The varied and sophisticated infrastructure of the school is significant. The Campion multimedia theater is armed with all modern instructional aids like CD ROMS, videotapes and audio cassettes. Different venues like the Campion Amphitheatre, the Gerard Manley Hopkins Auditorium, and its Annex halls provide ample opportunities and scope for students in public speaking, debate, drama, dance, yoga etc.






school bus conveyance

It is so much fun to go on a school bus!

School has an elaborate commuting network with buses plying from the different places of the city to the school. Whenever students cannot be brought back home safely by school bused either due to traffic-jam on the route, caused by political gathering in the city, or due to any kind of unrest or strike, bandh or hartal, they will be brought back to the school and made to remain safely on the school campus under the protection of the school security staff. On such occasions, parents are to get in touch with the school and collect their wards from the school itself.

what's new

  • seminar on ‘nasha mukt bharat’   17 February 2021

    “Mistakes are bound to happen. Everyone makes them. Every time you experience a relapse, tell yourself it is not over yet. Pick yourself up and try one more time.”


    Campion school in association with the Social Justice Department, Ernakulam conducted a very constructive seminar on the topic ‘Nasha Mukt Bharat’ (Drug-free India), a collaborative endeavour taken up by the Government of India, aiming at generating awareness about the ill-effects of drugs especially among the youth, for the students of classes IX, XI and VIII on 17, 19 and 20 March respectively.

    The Director of the school, Dr. K.V. Thomas was the Chief Guest on 17th and the Dean and Senior Principal Dr. Leelamma Thomas presided over the programme in the following days. Resource person of the day Adv. Hema V. K., detailed on the concept of substance abuse, the effect it has on a person both socially, and emotionally.

    Citing reasons why people take drugs, she elaborated that there are multiple reasons for substance addiction, the most important one being the elevated pleasure derived from the substance which ultimately results in addiction.

    On management and prevention perspective, she mentioned that awareness generation is the best key and she also added that we must have a positive attitude towards serving the society by reaching out to help those in need of dedication. She also extended some discrete and confidential helpline numbers to the students in case they want to reach out. The informative session rounded off at 12.40 PM.

  • republic day celebration

    With freedom in the mind, strength in the words, pureness in our blood, pride in our souls, zeal in our hearts, let’s salute our India on the Republic Day 2021.26th of January is a proud day for every Indian. On 26th January 1950, the Constitution of India came into force and our country, India, became the Republic. We celebrate this day with all its solemnity and grandeur.

    On the auspicious day of 26th January 2021, our school celebrated the 72nd Republic Day with full vigor and enthusiasm through virtual platform.

    Various activities such as poster making, speeches, quizzes and messages on Republic Day were conducted in which students participated with great patriotism.The video featured the recollection of the first Republic Day, recalling the true Heroes of India who had made a free India. The video also shows various scientific and technological developments took place in India since 1950.

    Student's presented the various traditional dressing styles in different state of India as a part of cultural heritage and national integration. In fact, it was a day infused with Patriotism and love for India.

Mary Poonen Lukose

Mary Poonen Lukose

Mary Poonen Lukose was an Indian gynecologist, obstetrician and the first female Surgeon General in India. She was the founder of a Tuberculosis Sanatorium in Nagarcoil and the X-Ray and Radium Institute, Thiruvananthapuram, served as the head of the Health Department in the Princely State of Travancore and was the first woman legislator of the state. The Government of India awarded her the fourth highest Indian civilian award of Padma Shri in 1975.
